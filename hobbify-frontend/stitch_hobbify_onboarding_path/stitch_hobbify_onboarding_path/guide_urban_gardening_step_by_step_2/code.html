Choose Your Containers

Success in urban gardening starts with where your plants live. Since space is at a premium, the right vessel isn't just about aesthetics—it's about survival.

info Breathable Material

The Golden Rule: Drainage

Never, under any circumstances, use a container without drainage holes. Without them, water settles at the bottom, creating a "drowning" environment for roots. If you find a perfect pot without holes, you must drill them yourself using a masonry bit.

  • check_circle Terracotta: Breathable and great for plants that like dry soil (like succulents or herbs).
  • check_circle Plastic/Resin: Lightweight and retains moisture longer. Ideal for high-rise balconies where wind dries things out fast.
  • check_circle Fabric Bags: Excellent for root health as they "air prune" the roots and prevent circling.

auto_awesome Pro-Tip: The Finger Test

"Before choosing a pot size, consider the 'Two-Inch Rule.' Your pot should be roughly 2 inches wider than the root ball of the plant you're moving into it. Too much extra soil holds unnecessary water and can lead to rot."
